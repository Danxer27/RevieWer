# 1. Summary of the Paper

This manuscript proposes a significant shift in the peer review process, moving from a largely opaque system to one where authors can publish their reviewer comments alongside their papers.  The core idea is to create a publicly accessible record of the entire review process, fostering greater transparency and potentially improving the reliability of scientific findings.  The paper details a pilot program initiated by Nature Communications in 2016, expanding to several journals, and highlights the benefits of this open approach.  The authors argue that this transparency strengthens the scientific record, facilitates scholarly discussion, and provides authors with valuable insights into the review process itself.

# 2. Novelty and Significance

The novelty lies in the proactive publication of reviewer comments – a practice largely absent in traditional peer review.  While reviewers often document their feedback, the current system lacks a publicly available, chronological record.  The paper’s significance stems from the potential to:

*   **Enhance Transparency:**  Increase public scrutiny of the peer review process, potentially identifying biases or inconsistencies.
*   **Improve Reliability:**  Provide a more comprehensive and readily accessible record of the review process, aiding in the identification of potential flaws or limitations.
*   **Foster Scholarly Discussion:**  Create a platform for discussion and debate surrounding the review process, potentially leading to improvements.
*   **Increase Author Engagement:**  Offer authors a valuable opportunity to engage with the reviewers and potentially refine their work.
